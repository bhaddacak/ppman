\chapter{Verbs}

Verbs listed here come from CPED. Fortunately, the dictionary has already provided us the composition of verbs and their related forms. New students can learn a lot just by going through these lists.

The verb list is roughly divided into two groups: (1) \emph{Main}, the verbs' citation form (singular, present, third-person, active), typically those ending with \pali{-ti}, and (2) \emph{Other}, the rest. In the former, you can also filter the list by the verbs themselves, their root and prefix, their termination (\pali{paccaya})\footnote{The name of roots and \pali{paccaya}s can be slightly different from Kaccāyana/Saddanīti's convention.}, or their meaning. The use is straightforward. Figure \ref{fig:verbs-main} shows a search result.

In other verb forms, you have to choose the group you want (see Figure \ref{fig:verbs-other}). The names of verb forms here come directly from the dictionary. So, some name may look unfamiliar, for example, \emph{Potential Participle} is called commonly by other textbooks as \emph{Future Passive Participle}.

If you cannot find some verbs, or some forms look dubious to you, please check them against DPD (see Chapter \ref{chap:dpd}) or MDPD in a Pāli Dict window, which has a more exhaustive list.
